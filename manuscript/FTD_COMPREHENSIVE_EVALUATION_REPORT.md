## CRITICAL FINDINGS (Revised)

### Legitimate Concerns (Not Fatal Flaws)

1. **Constraint Class Selection**: The Fibonacci skeleton constraints are argued from self-reference principles, not proven to be the unique possible constraints. This is acknowledged explicitly in the manuscript.

2. **N_c = 3.024 Interpretation**: Taking floor(3.024) = 3 is legitimate as a selection mechanism, but the physical meaning of the non-integer value requires more development.

3. **Bell Theorem**: The sLoop mechanism's compatibility with Bell's theorem needs more rigorous treatment. The manuscript acknowledges this as a conjecture, not a theorem.

4. **Lorentz Invariance**: The cubic lattice does break Lorentz symmetry at the Planck scale. The relational reinterpretation is philosophically interesting but physically untested.

### What The Original Evaluation Got Wrong

5. **"Circular Derivation of Alpha"** — INCORRECT. The derivation chain is:
   - Fibonacci constraints → unique integers {7, 3, 13, 4}
   - Self-reference closure → 5-mode curve with power-of-2 frequencies
   - Integration → arc length L = 23.7996
   - Framework scaling → G* = 2.9587
   - Master quadratic → α = 1/137.036

   This is a constrained derivation, not circular reasoning.

6. **"Framework integers are inputs, not outputs"** — PARTIALLY INCORRECT. The integers are uniquely determined within the Fibonacci skeleton constraints. The constraints themselves are selections, but the integers are outputs of those constraints.

7. **"Sophisticated curve-fitting"** — UNFAIR. The manuscript explicitly states (lines 11-19 of Chapter 1.10): "The proper characterization is: self-consistent fit within a structured framework—more principled than pure numerology, less certain than a parameter-free prediction."
